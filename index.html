naim

naim is a console client for AOL Instant Messenger (AIM), ICQ, Internet Relay Chat (IRC), and The lily CMC.

The best just got better.

I know. It's the same story for all of us. naim's dead; long live naim. Mainline naim doesn't support OSCAR; it hasn't been updated in nearly three years (May 2009). We all thought the time of naim had come and gone.

But here we are -- it's 2012, but everything old is new again. It's still the same naim that you know and love. But now, it has the features you expect from a modern CMC client -- scripting support (all the way down to protocol drivers), support for OSCAR, connecting to IRC with passwords that work, and more. When your irssi-using friends make fun of you for not being able to automatically make rainbow-text in your client, you can tell them to shove it, because now you can too. When your Pidgin-using friend ask where you've been on AIM, now you can tell them -- the best is back.
